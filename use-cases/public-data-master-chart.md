# Public‑data AI use cases — master chart

What AI maps, and which public datasets ground it.

---

## 1. Climate and environmental risk

### Climate impact chains

- **What AI maps:** initial driver, thresholds, policy reactions, secondary effects  
- **Public data sources:** NASA, NOAA, USGS, NRCS, EPA  

### Local system resilience

- **What AI maps:** climate stress drivers, institutional responses, market signals, indicators  
- **Public data sources:** NOAA, state climate offices, utility filings  

### Flood risk and housing

- **What AI maps:** FEMA maps, insurance trends, property values, infrastructure spending  
- **Public data sources:** FEMA NFIP, state insurance filings, county assessor  

### Regional hazard exposure

- **What AI maps:** hazard type, infrastructure thresholds, response policies, economic ripple effects  
- **Public data sources:** NOAA, USACE, local planning documents  

### Environmental hazards

- **What AI maps:** Superfund sites, toxic releases, industrial permits, land‑use history  
- **Public data sources:** EPA Superfund, TRI, state DEQ  

### Air quality

- **What AI maps:** monitor readings, seasonal trends, industrial sources  
- **Public data sources:** EPA AirNow, state air‑quality reports  

### Water quality

- **What AI maps:** violations, utility reports, infrastructure age  
- **Public data sources:** EPA SDWIS, local water utilities  

### Fire risk

- **What AI maps:** historical perimeters, dryness maps, evacuation routes  
- **Public data sources:** USFS, CAL FIRE, state forestry  

### Weather trends

- **What AI maps:** historical patterns, extremes, seasonal shifts  
- **Public data sources:** NOAA climate normals  

### Corporate climate claims

- **What AI maps:** sustainability reports vs EPA GHG filings  
- **Public data sources:** EPA FLIGHT, corporate ESG reports  

### Home climate resilience

- **What AI maps:** heat projections, groundwater, soil strength  
- **Public data sources:** NASA, USGS, NRCS
