# Domain patterns

How the same reasoning pattern shows up across domains.

---

## Physical systems

- **Domain:** climate, flooding, fire, air, water, soil, heat, infrastructure thresholds  
- **Pattern:** drivers → thresholds → cascading effects  

You start from a physical driver (e.g., rainfall, heat, wind), identify thresholds (e.g., drainage capacity, road design limits), and then map the cascade (e.g., flooded homes, road closures, economic disruption).

---

## Infrastructure and utilities

- **Domain:** roads, transit, electric grid, broadband, water, stormwater  
- **Pattern:** performance → bottlenecks → failure modes  

You read performance metrics (e.g., outages, delays, congestion), find bottlenecks (e.g., undersized lines, missing routes), and identify failure modes (e.g., blackouts, stranded riders).

---

## Housing and land systems

- **Domain:** affordability, vacancy, permits, values, insurance, evictions, neighborhood conditions  
- **Pattern:** physical exposure → market signals → social outcomes  

You connect physical risk (e.g., flood zones, heat, fire) to market signals (e.g., prices, insurance, lending) and then to social outcomes (e.g., displacement, segregation, decline).

---

## Public safety and institutions

- **Domain:** policing, courts, emergency response, public budgets, local taxes  
- **Pattern:** resources → throughput → outcomes  

You track resources (staff, funding), throughput (cases, calls, response times), and outcomes (clearance rates, backlogs, trust proxies).

---

## Health and human systems

- **Domain:** hospital quality, community health, overdoses, life expectancy, vaccination  
- **Pattern:** baseline → deviation → systemic strain  

You establish baselines (normal rates), detect deviations (spikes, drops), and infer systemic strain (capacity, burnout, unmet need).

---

## Economy and labor

- **Domain:** business licenses, unemployment, vacancy, wages, consumer costs  
- **Pattern:** economic drift → pressure points → resilience  

You read slow shifts (drift), identify stress points (closures, arrears, layoffs), and assess resilience (diversity, buffers, adaptation).

---

## Education and social systems

- **Domain:** graduation, funding, teacher retention, enrollment  
- **Pattern:** inputs → stability → long‑term outcomes  

You map inputs (funding, staffing), stability (turnover, enrollment), and long‑term outcomes (completion, attainment, opportunity).

---

## Consumer and daily life

- **Domain:** seller trust, used cars, neighborhood exploration, trip planning, food access  
- **Pattern:** risk assessment → verification → decision clarity  

You identify risks, verify claims with public data, and arrive at a clear, documented decision.
