# Meta‑architecture: the reasoning engine

This file defines the core logic that powers every domain and use case.

---

## Step 1: Identify the driver

**Question:** What force is acting on the system?

Examples:

- A heatwave, flood, or drought  
- A budget cut or new funding stream  
- A plant closure, new employer, or zoning change  
- A policy shift, regulation, or court ruling  

You name the **primary push** on the system.

---

## Step 2: Identify the thresholds

**Question:** Where does the system bend, break, or shift modes?

Examples:

- River level at which roads or homes flood  
- Hospital occupancy where wait times spike  
- Grid load where outages become likely  
- Rent‑to‑income ratio where evictions rise  

You locate **points of nonlinearity**—where behavior changes.

---

## Step 3: Identify the institutional response

**Question:** What do governments, markets, or agencies do?

Examples:

- Evacuation orders, emergency declarations, curfews  
- Rate hikes, insurance withdrawals, new fees  
- Service cuts, hiring freezes, or surge staffing  
- Zoning changes, moratoria, or incentives  

You map **who reacts, how fast, and with what tools**.

---

## Step 4: Identify the secondary effects

**Question:** What cascades outward?

Examples:

- Displacement, rent spikes, or neighborhood decline  
- Business closures or new clusters of activity  
- Shifts in school enrollment or staffing  
- Changes in health outcomes or life expectancy  

You trace **ripple effects** across systems and time.

---

## Step 5: Anchor everything in public data

**Question:** What public datasets can verify each step?

Principles:

- Prefer **official, recurring** datasets (federal, state, local).  
- Use **multiple sources** to cross‑check stories.  
- Treat **AI as a reasoning layer**, not a source of facts.  

You ground the entire chain in **named, inspectable datasets**.

---

## Why this meta‑architecture is cross‑domain

Because every system has:

- A driver  
- Thresholds  
- Institutions  
- Cascades  
- Observable data  

You can apply this same scaffold to:

- Climate hazards  
- Infrastructure reliability  
- Housing markets  
- Courts and policing  
- Hospitals and health  
- Labor markets and wages  
- Schools and social stability  
- Consumer decisions and daily life  

The domains change.  
The logic does not.
